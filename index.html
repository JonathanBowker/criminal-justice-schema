    This is the documentation of JCS version 1.0.0.

    What is JCS?

    The Justice Common Schema (JCS) is an open-source, proposed model for a human-readable "taxonomy" of justice-related data types, behaviours, and usages. Developed with input from legal and technical experts in the AI Justice Community, JCS aims to establish a standardised, interoperable schema for managing criminal trial data.

    JCS is intended to facilitate the integration of Retrieval Augmented Generative Systems (RAGS) into the typical trail preparation process. By providing a unified framework, JCS ensures that justice-related data is consistent, interoperable, and easily accessible, allowing different systems and platforms to communicate effectively.

    Purpose of JCS

    The purpose of JCS is to develop a common schema that serves as a standard for structuring justice-related data. It aims to: - Standardize Data: Define a unified set of fields and data types used across criminal trial and legal data management systems. - Enhance Interoperability: Enable seamless data exchange and integration between different databases, systems, and applications, supporting AI and generative technologies. - Improve Usability: Create a human-readable and machine-friendly format, making it easier for legal professionals, data scientists, and developers to work with justice-related information efficiently. - Support RAGS: Develop a schema that supports the deployment of Retrieval Augmented Generative Systems into case preparation workflows, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of legal processes.

    Schema Structure

    JCS specifies field names and data types suitable for various justice-related applications, including criminal case management, legal evidence tracking, and court proceedings. It organizes these fields into levels that indicate the importance and expected usage of each field at different stages of a trial. This structured approach allows for flexible adaptation as cases or needs evolve.
